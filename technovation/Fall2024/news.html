Week 1 and 2

Technovation Kicked-off!

We kicked off the Fall 2024 version of Technovation on Saturday with enthusiastic students and incredible mentors — we hope your student is just as excited as we are for the coming weeks! We are so excited to introduce the world of coding to our students! During this course, we want all students to remember that they are learning something new, it might be challenging at times. But! — learning to overcome those challenges and how to have fun with it is just as important as learning the material.

Before we dive into a full recap of our first meeting, we've got a few announcements to share!

Announcements

    1. Mentors’ feedback is now published on CodeHS!

    After every lesson, mentors in Technovation will give constructive feedback on the assignment progress of their students. Make sure to check the feedback on D2L and come to our “bonus hour” (TBD) if there are any questions about the feedback!

    3. Notify us!

    If the student is unable to attend any meeting, please let us know before Saturday via technovation@egr.msu.edu!

    4. Looking Ahead

    We'll see you again this Saturday, 10/12 at 10:30a ET for our third Technovation meeting of the fall, where we will review last weeks material and begin learning some new material

    4. Questions?

    As always, please reach out with any questions / concerns / ideas / feedback to technovation@egr.msu.edu.

Meeting Recap

Our students wasted no time getting started, jumping fearlessly into introductions and icebreakers! Following a brief overview of Technovation and the plan for each weekly meeting this fall, students in each cohort collaborated to draft their very own club contract, laying out their shared expectations and goals towards which to strive this fall. Check out the contracts below—we're impressed with what our students came up with!

Week 1 - 09/28

Technovation kicked-off with a small meet the mentors session. We then moved on to create a Club Contract – rules we should all follow during the club meeting. A brief introduction to HTML and CSS – amazing tools for website development.

Week 2 - 10/05

We started our meeting with making our own nametags as an icebreaker activity. We then moved on to learn about tags that are used in HTML coding, small Code Along activities with the mentors, and finished the lesson off with Exercises from CodeHS!

In both days students concluded the session with a reflection and discussion around what went well, what didn't go so well, and what excites them about the week ahead—and just like that, our lessons were up! Time flies when we're having fun—we're already looking forward to next Saturday.

Fall 2024

We're excited to announce that the funding from the TechSmith Company and from Michigan State University College of Engineering will allow Technovation to serve this fall!

Founded in 2018, Technovation is an MSU student-led coding club introducing 6-12th grade students in Michigan to the wide world of computer science. Combining games and mini-projects with guest speakers and team-building activities, Technovation is more than just another extracurricular—it's a community. We believe coding should be fun, and we aim to make it so!

Join us as we embark on an 9-week adventure, and prepare to have a blast!

For more information, see our home page.

We'll see you soon. In the meantime, stay healthy, stay safe, and stay positive.
